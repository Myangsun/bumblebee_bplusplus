  ## Literature sources for partial rebalancing

  These are real, well-cited papers:
  1. Kang et al. (2020) "Decoupling Representation and Classifier for Long-Tailed Recognition"
  (ICLR 2020) — showed sqrt-balanced resampling of the classifier head outperforms full
  equalization
  2. Mahajan et al. (2018) "Exploring the Limits of Weakly Supervised Pretraining" (ECCV 2018) —
  square-root frequency sampling beat both uniform and natural frequency on Instagram-1B
  3. Liu et al. (2019) "Large-Scale Long-Tailed Recognition in an Open World" (CVPR 2019) — showed
  aggressive oversampling of tail classes causes overfitting by cycling through same small
  real-image pool

  These papers are about resampling strategies during training, not about synthetic augmentation
  volume specifically. The sqrt formula is principled for how often to show each class, but
  extrapolating it to "how many synthetic images to generate" is my inference, not a direct finding
   from these papers.
