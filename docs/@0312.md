## No-Background Test Results

**Hypothesis:** Yellow flowers visually compete with the yellow T4 tergite band, causing LLM judge failures.

**Method:** Removed backgrounds from all 1,100 synthetic images (650 ashtoni, 450 sandersoni) using Grounding DINO + SAM, composited onto white. Re-ran GPT-4o LLM judge.

**Result: Background removal did NOT help — hypothesis not supported.**

### Pass Rate Comparison

| Metric | Original | No-BG | Delta |
|--------|----------|-------|-------|
| Overall pass rate | 90.9% | 90.6% | -0.3% |
| B. ashtoni | 85.1% | 85.1% | 0% (72 flips each way) |
| B. sandersoni | 99.3% | 98.7% | -0.7% |

### Yellow Background Effect (B. ashtoni)

| Background | Original | No-BG | Delta |
|------------|----------|-------|-------|
| Yellow (goldenrod/dandelion, n=260) | 83.5% | 84.6% | +1.1% |
| Other (n=390) | 86.2% | 85.4% | -0.8% |

Yellow backgrounds show a tiny +1.1% improvement — far too small to explain the 15% failure rate.

### Why Background Removal Hurt

1. **`wrong_coloration` failures surged 55%** (277 → 428): White backgrounds expose color imperfections that natural scenes masked.
2. **Species-level identification collapsed** (771 → 541, from 70% to 49%): The judge uses habitat context for identification.
3. **Morphological scores dropped**: Abdomen banding 4.01→3.76, thorax 4.38→4.18, head 4.11→3.92.
4. **Segmentation artifacts**: `background_bleed` increased 2→18.

### Viewpoint Breakdown

| Viewpoint | Original | No-BG | Delta |
|-----------|----------|-------|-------|
| dorsal | 81.8% | 88.6% | **+6.8%** |
| three-quarter_posterior | 83.2% | 84.5% | +1.3% |
| lateral | 95.5% | 94.5% | -1.0% |
| three-quarter_anterior | 96.4% | 93.6% | -2.8% |
| frontal | 97.7% | 91.8% | **-5.9%** |

**Dorsal is the only viewpoint that benefited** — backgrounds genuinely occlude top-down banding. Frontal views lost the most context.

### Conclusion

The primary failure driver is **borderline coloration accuracy** in synthetic images, not background interference. The LLM judge relies on naturalistic context for species identification; removing it hurts more than it helps.
